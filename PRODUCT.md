# Product

## Register

product

## Users

College and high-school students who study from PDFs, Word documents, and notes. They work alone, often on a laptop or tablet, switching between reading, annotating, and testing themselves before exams. They are comfortable with web apps but expect clear feedback when uploads or AI calls fail.

## Product Purpose

Lumio turns passive document reading into active study. Students upload course materials, organize them in folders, highlight and take notes in one place, and use AI (via their own OpenRouter key) for summaries, quizzes, flashcards, cheat sheets, and document Q&A. Success means faster comprehension, retained notes, and measurable practice without leaving the app.

## Brand Personality

Bold, clear, and trustworthy with a tactile edge. NeoPro Brutalism: strong structure and confident actions, not playful chaos. Voice is direct and student-friendly: explain what happened, say what to do next, avoid corporate filler.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS dashboards (cream body, soft shadows, purple gradients, hero metrics)
- Marketing-site layouts inside the app shell (oversized display type, eyebrow on every section)
- Vague AI copy ("unlock insights", "supercharge learning")
- Fragile flows that hide errors behind "Something went wrong"
- Light-gray body text on tinted backgrounds that fails contrast

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — Structure (borders, panels, labels) should make tasks obvious; style supports scanning, not competition with content.
2. **Practice what you teach** — Study tools must feel reliable: loading, empty, error, and disabled states on every core flow.
3. **User-owned AI** — AI is powerful but optional; never imply we host models or store keys in plaintext; surface actionable errors for key, billing, and model issues.
4. **Respect the study session** — Minimize friction between read, annotate, and generate; preserve context (folder, file, panel tab) across actions.
5. **Accessible by default** — Keyboard paths, visible focus, labeled controls, and readable contrast are part of shipping, not a later pass.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for text contrast, focus visibility, and form labels. Support `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations. Icon-only controls require accessible names. Do not rely on color alone for state (quiz correctness, highlights, errors).
